About Railroad Street Ukuleles

Following the innovations of American guitar makers of the late 20th century, Railroad Street ukuleles are thoroughly modern in construction and built of traditional materials. They are available with both jazz-style radiused fret board and traditional flat fretboard. I use Honduras Mahogany, Sapele and Koa for bodies, Western Red Cedar for tops, Mahogany for necks, Ebony and Rosewood for fret boards and pegheads. In addition to traditional pearl inlays, many Railroad Street Ukuleles have tinted resin inlays and other decorative detail. My ukuleles are primarily tenor size, though I'm working on producing a concert size in the near future.
